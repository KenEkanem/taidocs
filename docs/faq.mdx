Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Here are answers to some of the most common questions about Trust and Identity Solutions.

---

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Q: Is NINProfile mandatory for all Nigerian citizens?">
    <p>
    A: While the National Identification Number (NIN) itself is mandatory for all Nigerian citizens, using our NINProfile service is optional unless it's a specific requirement for services or platforms you wish to access that integrate with NINProfile.
    </p>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Q: How secure is my data with Trust and Identity solutions?">
    <p>
    A: We prioritize the security and privacy of your data. We utilize military-grade encryption (AES-256) for data at rest and in transit, strictly comply with the Nigeria Data Protection Regulation (NDPR), and implement robust access controls to protect your information from unauthorized access. 
    </p>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Q: Can small businesses use NINComply?">
    <p>
    A: Yes, absolutely\! Our NINComply solutions are designed to be scalable and are suitable for businesses of all sizes, from small enterprises to large corporations. We can tailor compliance solutions to fit your specific needs. 
    </p>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Q: How long does ReferenceMe keep employee records?">
    <p>
    A: The retention periods for employee records within ReferenceMe vary depending on the industry and can be configured by account administrators to comply with relevant regulations and organizational policies. 
    </p>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Q: What if I don't have a NIN yet?">
    <p>
    A: The National Identification Number (NIN) is foundational to our solutions. If you do not yet have a NIN, you will need to register for one with the National Identity Management Commission (NIMC) first before you can effectively use our Trust and Identity Solutions. 
    </p>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
