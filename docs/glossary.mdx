---
title: "Glossary"
---

Understanding key terminology is essential when working with digital identity and compliance solutions. Here's a glossary of terms frequently used within Trust and Identity Solutions and the broader Nigerian digital identity ecosystem.

---

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="NIN (National Identification Number)">
    <p>
    Nigeria's unique national identification number assigned to every citizen and legal resident. It serves as a foundational digital identity.
    </p>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="SSO (Single Sign-On)">
    <p>
    An authentication scheme that allows a user to log in with a single ID and password to gain access to multiple related, yet independent, software systems.
    </p>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="NDPR (Nigeria Data Protection Regulation)">
    <p>
    Nigeria's primary data privacy law, governing the collection, storage, processing, and transfer of personal data within Nigeria.
    </p>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="NIMC (National Identity Management Commission)">
    <p>
    The Nigerian government body responsible for managing and regulating the National Identity Management System, including the issuance of NINs.
    </p>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="KYC (Know Your Customer)">
    <p>
    The process of verifying the identity of clients and assessing their suitability, along with the potential risks of illegal intentions, before establishing a business relationship.
    </p>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="2FA (Two-Factor Authentication)">
    <p>
    A security method that requires two different methods of verification to confirm a user's identity, adding an extra layer of protection beyond just a password. Often interchangeable with MFA (Multi-Factor Authentication).
    </p>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="API (Application Programming Interface)">
    <p>
    A set of rules, routines, and protocols for building software applications. It specifies how software components should interact.
    </p>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="E-signature (Electronic Signature)">
    <p>
    An electronic symbol or process attached to or logically associated with a record and executed or adopted by a person with the intent to sign the record, carrying legal validity.
    </p>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
